What It Argues

Why higher education needs a new conversation

The forthcoming book argues that higher education can no longer rely on fragmented initiatives to respond to mounting pressure. Institutions need a more disciplined conversation about the business model, a more mature understanding of shared governance, and a more coherent system for institutional innovation.

Who It Is For

Built for leadership, not just commentary

This is a forthcoming playbook for leaders who must connect mission, governance, strategy, finance, enrollment, and innovation into a workable institutional response. It is designed to be read by presidents, provosts, trustees, deans, governance leaders, and strategic administrators.

Core Argument
Innovation is not an event. It is a system.
Shared governance is not a barrier. It is the engine.
Business model redesign is not a threat to mission. It is how mission endures.
Why It Matters Now

From pressure to redesign

The book is grounded in the reality that the sector is confronting demographic change, financial strain, technological disruption, alternative providers, changing employer expectations, regulatory burden, student debt, and declining trust. The question is no longer whether change is needed, but how it should happen.
